Understanding Window Performance Ratings

U-Factor (Insulation)

Measures how well the window prevents heat from escaping. Lower numbers are better. Our windows typically achieve U-factors below 0.30, meaning excellent insulation in Missouri's variable climate.

Solar Heat Gain Coefficient (SHGC)

Shows how much solar heat passes through the window. In Missouri, we recommend windows with moderate SHGC (around 0.30-0.40) to balance heat gain in winter with cooling in summer.

Visible Transmittance (VT)

The amount of visible light that passes through. Our windows maintain high VT (typically 0.50-0.60) so you get plenty of natural light while still enjoying energy savings.

Air Leakage

Measures how much air passes through the window. Our professionally installed windows achieve very low air leakage rates, keeping drafts out and your HVAC system working efficiently.
